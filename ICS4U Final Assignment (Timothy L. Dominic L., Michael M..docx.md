Deflation happens when prices go down due to low economic supplies and too much goods and services. Price stability is optimal and happens around an inflation rate of 1-3% Inflation affects everyone and everywhere Eroded Purchasing Power: Income buys fewer goods. Money Illusion: Focusing on nominal wage increases instead of real purchasing power. Lifestyle Creep: Reluctance to downgrade standard of living when prices rise. Sticky Habits: Maintaining spending by draining savings or accumulating debt. Reactive Panic: Fear-driven hoarding that worsens personal financial strain. Income adjustments falling behind rapid price spikes. Inflation Expectations: Businesses and workers preemptively raise prices and wages. This creates a self-fulfilling wage-price spiral that accelerates inflation. Supply Shocks: Sudden disruptions (e.g., oil spikes, shipping bottlenecks) cause rapid price jumps. This increases living costs while slowing economic output.Money Supply & Interest Rates: Rapid cash injection or low interest rates increase consumer demand. If demand outpaces production, it triggers demand-pull inflation. Central Bank Action: Central banks (like the Bank of Canada) raise interest rates to deliberately cool demand and slow inflation down.  Low-income households suffer most because essential goods (food, housing) consume a larger share of their income. Economic uncertainty, high unpredictable inflation slows long term plans. |

| **Summary** Inflation leads to a devaluing of currency, driving up the cost of everyday goods and services while the average person's wages fail to keep up. Because an individual or small group lacks the institutional power to reverse systemic inflation on a macroeconomic level, action must be focused on shifting consumer behavior. Large corporations frequently exploit inflationary pressures as a cover for corporate profiteering, compounding financial hardships and negatively impacting both the physical and mental health of vulnerable demographics.  To counter these pressures, we can implement disincentives against anti-consumer behavior and reward consumers for making financially resilient decisions. This can be effectively achieved by developing a budgeting application. By setting up profit/expense monitoring, inflation monitoring, and engaging with budgeting practice modules, users can steer clear of volatile financial environments, effectively manage their baseline cost of living, and alleviate financial anxiety.  |  |
